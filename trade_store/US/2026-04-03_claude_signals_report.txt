### 2. **Negative Alpha Epidemic: Strategy Underperformance vs. Passive Holding**
**Finding:** 43 signals (31%) show negative CAGR alpha, meaning the tactical timing strategy underperformed simple Buy & Hold over the backtested period.

**Examples:**
- **WIPRO.NS** (3 Monthly TRENDPULSE Longs): -27.6% to -37.51% MTM, deeply underwater
- **AMZN** (Monthly/Quarterly TRENDPULSE): -1.82% to -7.76% CAGR alpha despite 86.67%–100% win rates

- **PERSISTENT.NS** (Daily DELTADRIFT): -20.05% CAGR alpha (severe underperformance)
- **EEM** (Monthly TRENDPULSE): -2.63% CAGR alpha despite 83.33% win rate

**Implication:** High historical win rates (70%–100%) do **not guarantee outperformance** vs. passive strategies. Negative alpha signals suggest:
- **Timing inefficiency** (entries/exits capture less upside than holding)
- **Transaction costs** or **slippage** may erode gains
- **Market regime changes** (strategies optimized for past conditions may not persist)

**Recommendation:** Exclude all negative-alpha signals from authorized risk until model recalibration or market conditions shift.

### 3. **Lateness and Adverse Movement: Timing Decay**
**Finding:** 25 signals (18%) are late due to:
- **Time decay:** Elapsed holding periods exceed historical averages by 50%–400%
- **Price progress:** Current price has moved 60%+ toward first target
- **Adverse movement:** Price moved against signal direction by 3%–8% within days of trigger

**Examples:**
- **000660.KS** (Monthly FRACTAL TRACK Short): +8.55% adverse movement in 3 days (price rose when short signal triggered)
- **WPM** (Monthly FRACTAL TRACK Short): +3.47% adverse movement in 3 days
- **CSCO** (Monthly FRACTAL TRACK Short): +1.84% adverse movement, but waived by exceptional 62.8× RR
- **EBO.NZ** (Weekly DELTADRIFT): 72% progress toward T1 in 5 days (too fast)

**Implication:** Signal deterioration occurs rapidly. Even "fresh" signals (0–5 days old) can become stale if price moves adversely or too quickly toward targets.

**Recommendation:** Implement **real-time monitoring** with automatic disqualification for:
- Adverse movement >2% within first 3 days
- Progress >50% toward T1 within first week
- Elapsed time >150% of historical average holding period

**Finding:** 12 signals (9%) face directional conflicts or recent exits that reduce authorization confidence.

### 7. **Risk-Reward Asymmetry: Wide Stops, Modest Targets**
**Finding:** 34% of signals have **unfavorable risk-reward ratios** (<1.5×) due to:
- Wide stops (20%–40% below entry for Longs, above for Shorts)

- Missing or unreliable stop-loss levels (e.g., "No Recent Extrema," stops 50%+ away)

**Examples:**
- **GS** (Weekly FRACTAL TRACK): Stop at 782.21 (~9.4% below current 863.04), T1 at 865.11 (+0.2%), RR ≈0.02×
- **JPM** (Weekly FRACTAL TRACK): Stop at 210.28 (~28% below current 294.6), T1 at 313.73 (+6.5%), RR ≈0.23×

**Implication:** Most signals require **risking 5%–10%** to capture **3%–8% gains**, violating standard 2:1 RR thresholds. Only exceptional cases (CSCO 62.8×) justify entry.

**Recommendation:** Require **minimum 2.0× RR to first target** for Tier A qualification. Use tighter, technically validated stops (recent swing highs/lows, not distant F-Stack levels).

### 8. **Multi-Confirmation Illusion: Quantity ≠ Quality**
**Finding:** Assets with 2–3 "confirming" signals often show:
- **Conflicting timeframes** (Daily signal fresh, Quarterly signal 2 years old and at-target)
- **Redundant functions** (multiple TRENDPULSE signals on same interval, same direction)
- **Negative alpha on some confirmations** (one signal +5% alpha, another -7% alpha)

**Examples:**
- **JPM** (3 Long signals): Weekly FRACTAL TRACK fresh (+6.16% alpha), but 2 Quarterly TRENDPULSE signals are 1–1.8 years old, at-target, with -0.84% alpha
- **IFT.NZ** (3 Long signals): Weekly FRACTAL TRACK fresh (+5.53% alpha), but 2 Daily TRENDPULSE signals already exited 2026-03-31
- **PPH** (2 Long signals): Both Daily/Monthly at-target with minimal alpha (+0.12%, +0.56%)

**Implication:** Multi-confirmation is **misleading** when:
- Older signals are stale/at-target
- Different functions have conflicting alpha profiles
- Signals are highly correlated (same direction, overlapping timeframes)

**Recommendation:** Count only **distinct, fresh, positive-alpha signals** as true confirmations. Exclude stale/at-target/negative-alpha signals from confirmation counts.
