### 7. **Risk Management Framework: Stops, Targets, and Position Sizing**

**Stop-loss hierarchy** (use in order of availability):
1. **Recent Extrema** (most responsive to current price action)
2. **F-Stack 1** (Fibonacci-based support, typically 5-15% below entry)
3. **Horizontal Support** (long-term structural levels)
4. **EMA 200** (dynamic trend filter)
5. **Cancellation Level** (for unconfirmed signals only; invalidation point)

**Target hierarchy** (take partial profits at each level):
1. **T2 (Avg % Gain of Historic Winning Trades)** — most conservative, typically 2-10% gain
2. **T1 (Historic Rise to Pivot)** — slightly more aggressive, typically 5-15% gain
3. **Function Specific Target** — model-specific projection, varies widely
4. **F-Stack 1/2** — Fibonacci extensions, 15-50% gain
5. **Horizontal Resistance** — long-term structural targets

**Position sizing** (based on win rate and CAGR alpha):
- **Tier 1 (5-10% portfolio weight)**: Win rate ≥90%, CAGR alpha ≥+5%, multi-confirmation (e.g., AVGO, MCY.NZ, BAC FRACTAL TRACK Weekly)
- **Tier 2 (3-5% portfolio weight)**: Win rate 80-90%, CAGR alpha +2-5%, single or dual confirmation (e.g., NFLX, ETH-USD, HDB)
- **Tier 3 (1-3% portfolio weight)**: Win rate 70-80%, CAGR alpha 0-2% or negative alpha with high win rate (e.g., EEM, JETS, ATZ.TO)
- **Avoid >3% weight**: Signals with negative CAGR alpha <-10% (e.g., ATZ.TO -37.55%) unless purely tactical with tight stops
